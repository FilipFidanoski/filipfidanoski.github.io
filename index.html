About

I am an experimental and behavioural economist. My research studies how people make decisions under risk, uncertainty, ambiguity, time delay, and responsibility for others. I use laboratory and field experiments to examine individual and delegated decision-making, with applications to climate choices, algorithmic delegation, and public policy.

I hold a PhD in Economics from the School of Economics, UNSW Business School, University of New South Wales. My dissertation was titled Essays on risk and time preference elicitation.
