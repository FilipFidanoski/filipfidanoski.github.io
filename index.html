About

I am an experimental and behavioural economist. My research studies how people make decisions under risk, uncertainty, ambiguity, time delay, and responsibility for others. I use laboratory and online experiments, incentivised decision tasks, and behavioural models to examine individual and delegated decision-making, with applications to climate choices, algorithmic delegation, and public policy.

I hold a PhD in Economics from the School of Economics, UNSW Business School, University of New South Wales. My dissertation was titled Essays on risk and time preference elicitation.

🇲🇰 Nationality: Macedonian 🇨🇳 Chinese work permit Based in Hangzhou

Research Interests

Experimental and Behavioural Economics

My work uses incentivised laboratory and online experiments to study decision-making under risk, uncertainty, ambiguity, beliefs, time delay, and social responsibility. A central methodological concern across my projects is credible, reproducible, and robust behavioural measurement.

Projects and Research in Progress

🌍

Climate Choices and Decisions for Others

Laboratory and online experiments on decision-making for others under ambiguity and risk in the climate-change domain; climate choices, paternalism, preference projection, responsibility, aggregation, incentives, and the resolution of uncertainty.

⏳

Latent Risk and Time Preferences

Risky intertemporal choices, latent risk preferences, temporal stability, Bayesian analysis, preference consistency across contexts and domains, and experimental elicitation methodology.

🤖

Algorithmic Delegation and AI

Delegation to algorithmic and human forecasters, error magnitude and frequency, expected payoffs, comparative evaluation, energy decisions, and online experimental designs with incentivised choices.

🧪

Experimental Methodology

Laboratory and online experiments, incentive-compatible elicitation, experimental programming, preference measurement, robustness checks, reproducibility, credibility, and graphical versus visual presentation of the DEEP method.

📚

Meta-Analysis and Evidence Synthesis

Meta-analysis of latent risk preferences across contexts, bibliometric analysis, and survey work on elicitation methods, preference stability, robustness, credibility, and replication.

🎯

Persuasion and Strategic Behaviour

Optimal persuasion in the laboratory and experimental work on strategic behaviour, persuasion, beliefs, information, mechanism design, and decision environments.

📈

Corporate Takeovers and Premia

Experimental insights into takeover premia and the behavioural dynamics underlying corporate takeover decisions.
